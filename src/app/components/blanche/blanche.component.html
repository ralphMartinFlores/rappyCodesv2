At iFormatLogic, I created a landing page for Blanche, a high-quality cotton products seller. I started with a Photoshop design and turned it into HTML and CSS code. To keep the design accurate, I used Avocode to extract assets from the PSD file, making the conversion process smoother.

Objectives & Goals

The PSD included a blurred image that perfectly matched the top part of the sofa. This helped me pinpoint the exact area I wanted to blur within the hero section image. I also used this opportunity to refine my absolute positioning skills, which I found to be the best approach for implementing the call-to-action element.

Problems and Thought Process

Due to time constraints on the project, I opted for a different approach. To make room for the hero text, I added a blur effect to the top of the sofa using Adobe Photoshop. Also, I received several revision requests about text sizes being too large. To address this, I switched to using pixel measurements for font sizes instead of ems, successfully resolving the issue.

Lessons Learned

This project allowed me to learn new skills, such as selectively blurring parts of an image using Photoshop. Additionally, I gained insight into the importance of carefully examining PSD designs when converting them into code. It highlighted the need to find the right balance in font sizes, ensuring they are readable while accommodating the preferences of the majority of users.
